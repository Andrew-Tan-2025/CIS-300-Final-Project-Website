Hades

Hades is SuperGiant's most well-known titles, and is the first game I officially got into. While not the most innovative mechanically, Hades makes it's mark by having fantastic dialogue & character writing, creating its own interpretation on Greek Mythology.

Gameplay

Hades is a roguelite, a subgenre of roguelike games that allows for some level of permanent progression. The permanent progression takes the form of the Mirror of Night, which offers stat upgrades & new abilities(second dash, more lives per run, etc.), and the permanent upgrades to the weapons, of which there are 6 categories with 4 variants per. Each run consists of entering & clearing a number of chambers, which offer a small reward/weapon upgrades/boons, which culminates to a Boss fight. This loop repeats for 4 areas, each with a new set of enemies & hazards.

While the implementation of roguelike mechanics(i.e. restart from beginning, select buffs acquired in a given run, etc.) are not unique, the progression of the main story & other character's stories is what makes Hades unique among roguelikes. The story and its many threads advance at different speeds, all dependent on your progression through the game, which in turn creates a wholly unique experience for every player. In addition, the player is able to interact with & gift a wild assortment of side characters, all from Greek Mythology and each with their own compelling story & unique twist from their mythological counterparts.

Favorite Characters

Zagreus Nyx Thanatos
The son of Hades, Zagreus is the prince of hell and the protagonist of Hades. Zagreus is a lovable & earnest idiot, who cannot help but involve himself in other people's problems. Zagreus's commitment to other people's problems is why he is my favorite characters in Hades. Nyx is the "mother" of Zagreus, and an ally to his quest. She offers the Mirror of Night, which offers perma-upgrades, and reaches out to the Olympian Gods & Goddesses to aid Zagreus. Despite her stoic exterior, Nyx does truly care about Zagreus & openly loves him in her own way, which is why she is one of my favorites. She accepted the responsibility of raising Zagreus, and accepts him as her own. Initially a rival to Zagreus, Thanatos occasionally appears to challenge Zagreus to see who is better. Like his mother Nyx, Thanatos is a stoic character, but over time a softer, more reserved version is revealed: Thanatos only acts as an obstacle to Zagreus's journey because he is afraid that Zagreus, when he meets his family, will leave his Cthonic family behind. He also gives you a bat plush, which is also nice.
