Pyre

Pyre is, unfortunately, the game I have spent least amount of time with. Although I have played so little, I still enjoy this game for its unique spin on the sports games genre, and the fun & interesting characters taht you will meet on your journey to freedom.

Gameplay

A majority of the game does not take place in an arena, but in the overworld. You start with the option to select one of several locations, each having some potnetial reward. After the selection is made, you are able to purchase/sell items, read about the lore of the land, or practice for the Rite, the sports-like event that marks the end of a journey.

The goal of the Rites is simple: carry/shoot the orb into the enemy's goal until their flame's health reaches 0. You are permitted 3 of your band of characters to participate in the Rite, but you are only allowed to control 1 of them at a time. Each character projects an aura, a danger zone that kills any player that touches it, which can be increased in size when standing inside a teammate's aura OR cast out as a projectile. Each character species determines the aura size, cast method, aura projection, etc. Pyre's gameplay, while not as fast-paced as other sports games, is somehow a tense & exhilirating experience. The small gameplay changes emphasize the importance of positioning & team composition, and every teammate death means the enemy has the chance to score. The ability to construct a team and strategy, alongside the moment-to-moment plays that define sports games, is why I enjoy Pyre.

Favorite Characters

Jodariel Sandalwood Ti'zo
Jodariel is the muscle & protector of the group, who also steers the group out of danger with her knowledge of the Downside. This protector role she plays is also reflected in her gameplay, as she casts the largest aura of any character, with the capacity to shoot a large projectile capable of catching any unsuspecitng contestant with ease. However, what I really love about Jodariel is her backstory: Jodariel has been in the Downside longer than anyone in the Nightwings, and you spark that small hope of her being able to be set free from the Rites. Both the player and Jodi bring something to the table, and you both become a fiery force to save your group from the Downside. The former Reader to the Nightwings, Sandalwood serves as a mentor to the player & the master to a greater plan he has in store for the participants in the Rites. He starts as an untrustworthy ally, joining much later into the game & only the Bard to vouch for him. When he joined the party, I did not trust him at all. As the game progressed, however, I began to change my perspective on him. Before he was banished to the Downside, Sandalwood operated a printing press, and would spend his time printing, reading, & distributing books. For this offense he was banished, and his goal is to see everyone liberated from the Rites, by sending everyone back. It was at this moment that I loved this character: Sandalwood had slowly earned my trust by confiding in the team, and his altruistic & ambitious goal to see everyone free made him an admirable character. I love Ti'zo because he is the cute mascot character. He starts out with the group & is the lovable imp that makes cute chirping noises with a hint of a sassy personality. He's like if a bat inherited a cat's temperament, and I love him for that.
